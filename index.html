Tips & Reference

Must-Know Facts for the FL 2-14 Exam

Memorize these numbers, rules, and definitions. They appear on virtually every exam.

🔍
Critical Numbers to Memorize
31 days
Grace Period
Policy stays in force 31 days after missed premium. If insured dies during grace period → death benefit paid minus overdue premium.
14 days
Free-Look Period
Standard free-look for life policies. Full premium refund if returned within 14 days of delivery.
21 days
Free-Look (Replacement)
Extended to 21 days when the new policy replaces an existing one. Also 21 days for ALL annuity contracts in Florida.
2 years
Incontestability Period
After 2 years, insurer CANNOT contest the policy for misrepresentation. Fraud = no time limit (can void anytime).
2 years
Suicide Clause
If insured commits suicide within first 2 years → only premiums paid are returned. After 2 years → full death benefit paid.
3 years
Reinstatement Period
Lapsed policy can be reinstated within 3 years. Requires: proof of insurability + back premiums + interest.
6 months
Backdating Limit
Policy can be backdated up to 6 months to "save age" and lock in a lower premium. Back premiums must be paid.
6 months
Waiver of Premium Waiting
Must be disabled for 6 months before premiums are waived. After 6 months, all premiums waived retroactively from date of disability.
6 months
Cash Value Delay
Insurer may delay payment of cash value loans or surrender values for up to 6 months (not for premium purposes).
5 months
Social Security Disability Wait
Must be continuously disabled for 5 months before Social Security disability benefits begin.
31 days
Group Life Conversion
Terminated employee has 31 days to convert group life to individual policy. No evidence of insurability required.
90 days
AD&D Death Requirement
For Accidental Death & Dismemberment rider to pay, death must occur within 90 days of the covered accident.
59½
IRA / Qualified Plan Age
Withdrawals before age 59½ = ordinary income tax + 10% IRS early withdrawal penalty.
Age 73
Required Minimum Distribution
RMDs must begin by April 1 of the year after the participant turns 73 (SECURE 2.0 Act).
48 months
License Appointment Lapse
A FL agent's license terminates if they go 48 months (4 years) without being appointed by an insurer.
5 years
Record Keeping
FL agents must maintain transaction records at their place of business for at least 5 years.
30 days
Address Change Notice
Agent must notify the DFS of any change of address within 30 days.
30 days
Death Benefit Payment
Insurer must pay death benefit within 30 days of receiving valid proof of loss, or interest accrues.
50%
Controlled Business Limit
An agent cannot write more than 50% controlled business (insurance on self, family, or employer).
≤ $25
Rebating Exception
FL allows rebating only if offered to all in same actuarial class. Promotional items ≤ $25 in value are also permitted.
24 hours
CE Requirement
24 CE credit hours required every 2-year license period. Must include: 5 hrs FL law + 3 hrs ethics.
Age 100
Whole Life Endowment
A whole life policy "endows" at age 100 — cash value equals face amount. Either death benefit or endowment pays.
24 months
Terminal Illness (Accelerated DB)
To qualify for accelerated death benefit in FL, insured must have a life expectancy of 24 months or less.
2 of 6 ADLs
LTC Rider Trigger
Long-term care rider pays when insured cannot perform at least 2 of the 6 Activities of Daily Living, or has cognitive impairment.
Essential Vocabulary
Premium
The amount paid to keep a life insurance policy active.
Policyholder / Policyowner
The person who owns and controls the insurance policy. Has the right to change beneficiary, assign, take loans, and surrender.
Insured
The person whose life is covered. When they die, the death benefit is paid. May or may not be the policyowner.
Beneficiary
Person who receives the death benefit. Primary = first in line. Contingent = backup if primary dies first.
Death Benefit
Money paid to beneficiaries when the insured dies. Almost always received income-tax free.
Cash Value
Savings element that builds inside permanent policies (Whole Life, UL, VUL). Can be borrowed or withdrawn. Loans not taxable unless policy lapses.
Underwriting
Process the insurer uses to evaluate risk (health, age, lifestyle) before approving coverage and setting the premium.
Rider
Optional add-on that gives extra benefits (e.g., waiver of premium, AD&D, guaranteed insurability).
Representation
Statement on the application believed to be true. If materially false → insurer may void policy within contestability period.
Warranty
Statement guaranteed to be absolutely true. Stronger than a representation.
Concealment
Intentional withholding of material facts from the insurer. Can void a policy.
Fraud
Intentional deception for gain. Policy can be voided at ANY time — no time limit.
Twisting
Using misrepresentation to induce someone to replace an existing policy with one from a DIFFERENT insurer. Agent earns new commission.
Churning
Using a client's own policy cash value to fund a replacement policy with the SAME insurer, mainly for commissions. Customer may lose value.
Rebating
Giving anything of value not stated in the policy to induce purchase. Illegal in FL except: same actuarial class or items ≤ $25.
Sliding
Adding products to a policy without the applicant's knowledge or consent, then charging them for it.
Defamation
Making false statements about a competitor insurer's financial condition to injure them.
Coercion
Using threats or intimidation to force someone to purchase insurance. Unfair trade practice.
Fiduciary Responsibility
Agents must handle client funds honestly and in clients' best interests. Premiums held in fiduciary capacity.
Insurable Interest
Must exist at the TIME OF APPLICATION. You must have a financial or emotional stake in the insured's life. Does not need to continue after policy issued.
STOLI / IOLI
Stranger / Investor-Originated Life Insurance. Circumvents insurable interest laws. Illegal — violates legal purpose element of a contract.
Lapse
Policy ends because premiums weren't paid and no cash value covered it.
Surrender Value
What you receive if you cancel a permanent policy early. = Cash Value minus any surrender charges.
MEC (Modified Endowment Contract)
Life policy overfunded beyond the 7-pay test. Withdrawals taxed LIFO + 10% penalty before 59½. Death benefit still income-tax free.
1035 Exchange
Tax-free swap of one life/annuity policy for another. Must be carrier-to-carrier direct transfer to qualify.
Viatical Settlement
Sale of a life policy by a terminally/chronically ill insured (life expectancy ≤ 24 months) to a third party for more than CSV but less than face amount.
Primary Insurance Amount (PIA)
Formula used to calculate full Social Security retirement benefits. Full retirement age = 67 (born after 1960). Earliest = 62 (reduced). Latest = 70 (increased).
FICA Taxes
Fund Social Security. Employee + employer each pay 7.65% (6.2% OASDI + 1.45% Medicare). Self-employed pay both sides.

Annuity Quick Reference
Immediate Annuity
Payments begin within 12 months. Always funded with a single premium lump sum.
Deferred Annuity
Has accumulation phase before payouts begin. Can be fixed, variable, or indexed.
Fixed Annuity
Guaranteed minimum interest rate. Insurer bears investment risk.
Variable Annuity
Invested in separate accounts. Owner bears investment risk. Requires securities license.
Indexed (Equity Indexed) Annuity
Returns tied to S&P 500 (or similar). Floor = 0% (never lose principal). Cap limits upside gains.
Life Annuity (Straight Life)
Pays for annuitant's lifetime only. No residual to beneficiary. Cannot be outlived.
Life with Period Certain
Pays for life, guaranteed for a minimum period (e.g. 10 or 20 years). If annuitant dies early, beneficiary gets remaining period.
Installment Refund
If annuitant dies before receiving full contract value, beneficiary gets remaining value in installments.
Flexible Installment Deferred
Multiple varying contributions over time. Recognizable by "no less than $X quarterly/monthly."
Standard Provisions — Must Know
Entire Contract Clause
Policy + attached application = the entire contract. Insurer cannot refer to outside documents. Only insurer executive can make written changes.
Incontestability Clause
After 2 years, insurer CANNOT contest the policy for misrepresentation. Fraud has NO time limit.
Grace Period
31 days after premium due date. Policy stays in force. If insured dies during grace period → benefit paid minus overdue premium.
Free-Look Period
14 days (21 if replacing). Starts at delivery. Return for full premium refund. Annuities = always 21 days in FL.
Reinstatement
Lapsed policy back in force within 3 years. Requires: proof of insurability + back premiums + interest. Advantage: premium based on younger age.
Automatic Premium Loan (APL)
Automatically borrows from cash value to pay overdue premium. Prevents lapse. Only works if policy has cash value (not term).
Misstatement of Age
If age was understated on application, insurer adjusts death benefit to what the premium would have purchased at correct age. Does NOT void the policy.
Suicide Clause
Within 2 years of issue: only premiums returned. After 2 years: full death benefit paid. Protects insurer from anti-selection.
Common Disaster Clause
If insured and primary beneficiary die in same accident and order cannot be determined → primary beneficiary is assumed to have died first → proceeds go to contingent beneficiary.
Spendthrift Clause
Protects proceeds from beneficiary's creditors. Beneficiary cannot assign or transfer payments.
Collateral Assignment
Temporary transfer of policy rights to a lender as loan collateral. Rights revert to owner when loan repaid. Used when policy is collateral for a bank loan.
Absolute Assignment
Permanent transfer of ALL ownership rights. New owner gets full control.
Payor Provision / Payor Clause
On juvenile policies: if the adult payor dies or becomes disabled, premiums are waived. Covers the CHILD's life; the payor is the adult.
Waiver of Premium Rider
If insured becomes totally and permanently disabled: premiums waived. 6-month waiting period, then waived retroactively. Available until age 60–65. Does NOT require Social Security disability approval.
Guaranteed Insurability Rider (GIR)
Purchase additional coverage at specified future dates WITHOUT evidence of insurability. Options at ages 25, 28, 31, 34, 37, 40 or life events (marriage, birth).
Accelerated Death Benefit (Living Benefit)
Terminally ill insured (life expectancy ≤ 24 months in FL) can access a portion of the death benefit early. Amount received is income-tax free. Reduces final death benefit.
Policy Loan
Only on permanent policies with cash value. Not taxable. If loan + interest exceeds cash value → policy lapses. Outstanding loan deducted from death benefit at death.
Nonforfeiture Options (Whole Life only)
Cash Surrender Value
Receive accumulated cash value as lump sum. Policy terminates. No more coverage.
Reduced Paid-Up Insurance
Use cash value to buy a smaller, fully paid-up whole life policy. No more premiums. Cash value continues to build. ✅ Only nonforfeiture option that continues cash value growth.
Extended Term Insurance
Use cash value to buy term insurance for the SAME face amount for as long as cash value allows. Default nonforfeiture option. No cash value growth.
Dividend Options (Participating Policies)
Cash
Dividend paid directly to policyowner. Dividends are NOT taxable (return of excess premium).
Reduction of Premium
Dividend applied to reduce next premium due.
Accumulate at Interest
Dividends left with insurer earning interest. Interest IS taxable.
Paid-Up Additions (PUAs)
Dividend buys small, fully paid-up additional whole life coverage. ✅ Increases BOTH death benefit AND cash value. Best for inflation protection.
One-Year Term
Dividend purchases a 1-year term insurance rider.
Florida-Specific Rules
DFS vs OIR
DFS (Dept of Financial Services) = headed by elected CFO. Handles licensing, consumer complaints, AML, unclaimed property, rehabilitation/liquidation of insolvent insurers. OIR (Office of Insurance Regulation) = headed by Insurance Commissioner. Regulates solvency, rates, and policy forms.
Domestic / Foreign / Alien
Domestic = incorporated in FL. Foreign = incorporated in another U.S. state. Alien = incorporated outside the U.S. All must be admitted (licensed) by the state where they do business.
Mutual vs Stock Company
Mutual = owned by policyholders, may pay dividends. Stock = owned by shareholders, profits go to shareholders.
Agent Licensing Requirements
Must be: ≥ 18 years old, FL resident (or qualifying non-resident). No high school diploma required. License renewed every 2 years. 24 CE hours required: 5 hrs FL law + 3 hrs ethics.
Variable Products Licensing
Must hold FL Life & Variable Contracts license (2-14) PLUS FINRA Series 6 or Series 7 registration. A prospectus must be delivered before or at sale.
NAIFA Code of Ethics
The National Association of Insurance and Financial Advisors' code of ethics is specifically incorporated into Florida insurance law.
Florida Guaranty Association (FIGA)
Protects policyholders if a licensed FL insurer becomes insolvent. Funded by assessments on other insurers. NOT for unlicensed/unauthorized insurers.
Replacement Rules
Agent must provide Notice Regarding Replacement at time of application. Replacing insurer notifies existing insurer within 5 days. Written comparison provided if requested. Purpose: prevent twisting and churning.
Group Life Eligibility
Full-time employees (>40 hrs/week) after probationary period CANNOT be excluded. Noncontributory (employer pays all) = 100% participation required. Contributory (employees pay some) = usually 75% participation required.
USA Patriot Act
Requires life insurers to establish anti-money laundering (AML) compliance programs. Enacted post-9/11 to detect and deter terrorism financing.
Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA)
Insurer must notify applicant in writing before ordering investigative consumer reports or inspection reports. Governs use of MIB data.
MIB (Medical Information Bureau)
Clearinghouse for medical data shared among insurers. Reports past health, drug use, lifestyle. Can only share with applicant's physician (not directly to applicant or to other insurers without authorization).
Key Person Insurance
Business = policyowner, premium payer, AND beneficiary. Premiums NOT tax-deductible. Death benefit received income-tax free. Cross-purchase: each partner owns policy on the others. Entity/Stock: business owns all policies.
Social Security — Key Facts
FICA: Employee + Employer each pay 7.65%. Disability = 5-month waiting period. Full retirement age = 67 (born after 1960). SS does NOT cover dismemberment. Federal Government does NOT fund SS (FICA taxes only).
Backdating
Policy can be backdated up to 6 months to lock in lower premium based on younger age. Must pay back premiums to backdated date.
Minimum Application Signing Age
In Florida, the minimum age to sign a life insurance application is 15 years old.
Common Exam Traps & Confusing Distinctions
Twisting vs Churning?
Twisting = switch to a DIFFERENT insurer using misrepresentation. Churning = replace with SAME insurer using client's own cash value.
Variable Life vs Variable Universal Life?
Variable Life = FIXED premium, guaranteed minimum death benefit. VUL = FLEXIBLE premium, NO guaranteed minimum death benefit.
Waiver of Premium vs Payor Provision?
Waiver of Premium = waives premium if the INSURED is disabled. Payor Provision = waives premium on a JUVENILE policy if the adult PAYOR dies or is disabled.
Revocable vs Irrevocable beneficiary?
Revocable = policyowner can change anytime, no consent needed. Irrevocable = cannot change without beneficiary's written consent. Irrevocable also limits policy loans and assignments.
When must insurable interest exist?
At the TIME OF APPLICATION only. It does NOT need to continue after the policy is issued. The named beneficiary still collects even if the relationship ends.
Policy loan is taxable?
NO — policy loans are generally NOT taxable. They become taxable only if the policy lapses with an outstanding loan. Outstanding loan at death = deducted from death benefit.
Dividends taxable?
NO — dividends are considered a RETURN OF EXCESS PREMIUM and are not taxable income. Interest earned ON dividends left with the insurer IS taxable.
Federal Government funds Social Security?
NO — Social Security is funded ONLY by FICA taxes from employees and employers. NOT by general federal government revenue.
20-Pay vs 30-Pay vs Straight Life — which has fastest cash value?
20-Pay = fastest cash value (premiums compressed into fewer years). Single Premium = highest INITIAL cash value but that's different. For "quickest accumulation" → 20-Pay Life.
What does the "Consideration clause" contain?
The amount of PREMIUM and when payments are due. NOT the death benefit amount or beneficiary rights — those are in other clauses.
What's the "Insuring clause"?
The insurer's basic PROMISE to pay the death benefit. The core of the contract.
Whole Life: what is NOT guaranteed?
DIVIDENDS are NOT guaranteed. Guaranteed: level premiums, death benefit, cash value accumulation.
Term vs Permanent: who can have Automatic Premium Loan?
ONLY permanent policies with cash value. Term has NO cash value → APL cannot apply to decreasing term.
What is NOT required in a group life policy?
Accidental Death & Dismemberment (AD&D) provision is OPTIONAL in group life. Grace period, conversion privilege, and incontestability ARE required.
Contingent beneficiary receives proceeds when?
ONLY when the primary beneficiary dies BEFORE the insured. If primary survives (even by a moment), primary collects everything.
Agent's primary duty during application process?
To the INSURANCE COMPANY (not the applicant). The agent is the insurer's representative.
When does coverage begin with a conditional receipt?
From the APPLICATION DATE — IF the applicant would have been approved under normal underwriting. Not guaranteed.
No initial premium paid — when does coverage begin?
When the producer DELIVERS the policy AND collects the initial premium. Must also get a statement of good health.
Misstatement of age discovered at claim?
Insurer ADJUSTS the death benefit (not void, not deny). Pays what the premium would have bought at the correct age.
What does the incontestability clause protect against?
After 2 years, insurer CANNOT contest for misrepresentation. But FRAUD has no time limit. And the suicide clause is a separate exclusion.
Reduced Paid-Up vs Extended Term — which builds cash value?
Only REDUCED PAID-UP continues to build cash value (it's a smaller whole life policy). Extended Term is pure term = no cash value.
Noncontributory vs Contributory groups?
Noncontributory (employer pays 100%) = 100% participation required. Contributory (employee shares cost) = typically 75% required.
High school diploma required for FL license?
NO — NOT required. Must be ≥ 18, FL resident, pass state exam. No diploma needed.
